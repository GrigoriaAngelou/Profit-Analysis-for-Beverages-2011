Profit Analysis for 2011

Coffee and tea are two beverages that are loved by many people around the world. The money spent for them is not that high in most of the country. So, how much profit is made for those products? The dataset that is used in this project contains information about sales values in dollars on American Stores between 2010 and 2011. Let’s check how well they perform in America.

Coffee performance explained:

For all the stores combined we can see that the total profit for coffee types is 44.125 $, where the 76% of this profit derives from Columbian coffee.
One more positive outcome is that expenses had a dicrease of 1,02%.
Moreover, profit was increased by 18% compared to the profit from previous year.
Most coffee lovers in USA, live in New York, Colorado and Illinois. (They produce together the 30% of the total profit). In monthly
Sales chart we can observe that July, August and September coffee profits are on the rise.

Espresso performance explained:

Espresso has more total sales than coffee but the profit is a little less because there are more expenses into it.
There is also a 18% increase compare to the year 2010.
California, Illinois and Colorando produced the 39% of the total profit.
43% of the total profit is produced by Decaf Espresso.
The monthly profit that each espresso category produce is mostly in the same prices, but there is a big increase of Regular Espresso and Caffe Mocha in October.

Tea performance explained:

We can observe a 18% of total profit increase here too.
Iowa, New York, California produced almost half of the total profit.
Darjeeling and Early Grey are the reason of the profit cause Green Tea produce negative profit, with October to be the most damaging for profit losses.

Herbal Tea performance explained:

Created more profit from the tea products.
California, Iowa and Illinois produced 45% of the total profit.
The less profitable product for Herbal Tea is Mint.
There is a significant dicrease after July for Lemon and Mint products that is reflected back after 2 months. Something that is not observed for the Chamomile products.
